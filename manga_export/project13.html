The 50% Off Battle 🇯🇵

A Japanese supermarket tradition.

#Shopping

Evening Routine

"Just grabbing dinner."

It's late evening in a Japanese supermarket. I was just casually looking for a bento box for dinner, ready to pay full price.

#Shock

The Sticker God

"Wait… 50% OFF?!"

Then, HE appeared. The staff member with the sticker gun. He slapped a bright red "Hangaku" (Half Price) sticker right in front of me.

#Strategy

Silent Battle

"It’s like a silent battle."

No one says a word.

Suddenly, the atmosphere changed. Housewives, salarymen, students—everyone moved in. Polite but fast. It was a game of speed and strategy.

#Victory

Mission Accomplished

"Dinner secured."

Half the price.

I managed to snag one! The thrill of the hunt made the meal taste even better. In Japan, timing is everything.

💡 Culture Note HANGAKU

"Hangaku" (半額) means half-price. Japanese supermarkets discount perishable food heavily in the evening to reduce waste. It's a popular way to save money!
