Why Are They Running? 🇯🇵

The dedication of Japanese train staff.

#Confusion

Sudden Sprint

"Why is he running?!"

You're standing on a platform, and suddenly a station attendant sprints past you. Is there an emergency? Did someone fall?

#Shock

The Countdown

"The train leaves in 30 seconds."

No emergency. He's just sprinting to check that the far end of the train is clear before departure. He has seconds to do it.

#Safety

Precision Safety

"They’re making sure everyone is safe."

And on time.

They point and call everything. "Doors clear. Platform clear." The running isn't panic; it's a calculated effort to maintain the world's strictest schedule safely.

#Respect

Responsibility

"It’s not rushing."

It’s responsibility.

As the train glides away exactly on the second, he bows. It's a level of professional pride and dedication that keeps Tokyo moving.

💡 Culture Note POINT AND CALL

"Shisa Kanko" (Pointing and Calling) is a safety method used in Japan. By physically pointing and vocalizing checks, errors are reduced by up to 85%!
