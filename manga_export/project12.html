Wait… It’s Free? 🇯🇵

A surprising detail in Japanese restaurants.

#Ordering

Just Sat Down

"Let’s order something to drink."

You sit down at a cozy authentic restaurant, expecting to order a soda or bottled water. You look at the menu, ready to pay.

#Surprise

Unordered Service

"Wait… I didn’t order water."

Suddenly, a server places a glass of water on your table. You didn't ask for it. Is this a mistake? Will they charge me?

#Disbelief

Everyone Gets One

"It’s free?"

And everyone gets it?

You look around. Everyone has one. It’s ice-cold, clean, and… completely free. It’s unlimited.

#Hospitality

Hospitality

"No extra charge."

Just hospitality.

In Japan, water (and often tea!) is served immediately as a welcoming gesture. It’s a standard of service, not an upsell.

💡 Culture Note OMIZU & OCHA

Almost all restaurants in Japan serve free water (O-hiyamizu) or hot tea (O-cha) as soon as you sit down. Refills are usually free, too!
