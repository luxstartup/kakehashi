Luxembourg has embraced green tea, but have you tasted the 'First Flush' (Shincha)? It is to tea what 'Beaujolais Nouveau' is to wine—a fresh, vibrant celebration of the new season. This Kyoto matcha isn't for baking; it is for pure enjoyment.

The 'Jade Foam'. Kyoto's 300-Year Legacy.

Cultivated in the misty hills of Uji. Ground by stone mills in total darkness. A sip of silence in a busy world.

🇯🇵 Keyword: Umami (旨味)

Often translated as 'savory', true Umami in Matcha is a deep, broth-like richness that covers the tongue. Low-grade matcha is bitter; premium matcha is sweet and savory, almost like a vegetable soup.

Shadowed for 20 Days

The secret to the vibrant green color is 'Oishita' (shading). By blocking sunlight for weeks before harvest, the tea leaves produce high chlorophyll and amino acids (Theanine), reducing bitterness and boosting the Umami flavor.
