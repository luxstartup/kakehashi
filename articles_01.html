Tokyo Station Best Sellers:
Top 3 Exclusive Souvenirs 2025

Tokyo Station is a labyrinth of sweets. With over 100 shops, what do locals actually buy?
Based on the latest sales data released by JR East, here are the top 3 "New & Exclusive" sweets you must try.

Published: Jan 23, 2026 | Source: Gransta Tokyo
No. 1
Shop: Jaga Boulda (Calbee+)

Jaga Boulda: Golden Consommé Flavor

A dream collaboration between "Calbee" (Japan's top potato chip maker) and "Tokyo Banana". This isn't your ordinary chip. It uses a special "KGT manufacturing method" to keep the powder on the thick-cut potato. The "Golden Consommé" flavor is supervised by "Onogi", a famous Japanese restaurant in Hiroo. It tastes like sophisticated chicken soup.

1,047 JPY (4 bags) Approx. 6.50 €
No. 2
Shop: COCORIS

Gianduja & Pistachio Pie

"COCORIS" is famous for its "Sand Cookies" with the girl logo, but their new Pie is taking over. You get two flavors: "Gianduja" (Hazelnut chocolate paste) and "Pistachio". Pistachio sweets are currently booming in Tokyo. If you love European nutty flavors, this is the best choice.

2,000 JPY (8 pcs) Approx. 12.50 €
No. 3
Shop: Ivorish

French Toast Financier (Vanilla & Milk)

"Ivorish" is a popular French Toast specialty store in Fukuoka and Tokyo. They turned their famous French Toast into a bite-sized "Financier" cake. This "Vanilla & Milk" flavor is exclusive to Tokyo Station. It's fluffy, buttery, and perfect for coffee time.

1,188 JPY (6 pcs) Approx. 7.40 €
